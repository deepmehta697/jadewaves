# Competitor Gap SEO

This brief connects Jade Waves' current search demand, competitor positioning, and the on-site SEO moves needed to win more global buyer queries.

## Search Console Takeaways

- Existing demand already includes market-intent searches such as `silica sand kenya` and country-plus-product pages like `silica sand maldives` and `bentonite suppliers in vietnam`.
- The homepage is already earning clicks, but historic country/product content generated meaningful impressions and clicks as well.
- This indicates Jade Waves should target `product + use case + exporter/supplier + destination-market` intent for global buyers, not just generic India-only terms.

## Competitor Signals

- Leads with Quartz and Feldspar manufacturing/export language.
- Uses scale and capacity language prominently.
- References clientele and application industries, but product depth is limited.

- Strong on Quartz/Feldspar specialization.
- Emphasizes mines, operations, monthly output, and application focus.

### Ashapura Minechem

- Wins on domain authority, mine ownership, volume, and global reach.
- Strong application structure for Bentonite.
- Uses claims around producer scale, reserves, and exports.

Official source:
- https://bentonite.ashapura.com/

### Shree Ram Kaolin

- Strong on long operating history, sample CTA, and industry list.
- Product mix overlaps with Silica Sand/China Clay.
- Uses trust signals like certification, countries served, and inquiry CTAs.

Official source:
- https://shreeramkaolin.com/

### Puresil India

- Product portfolio is clean and focused.
- Strong brand naming, but product cards appear lighter on technical buyer detail.

Official source:
- https://puresil.in/products/

## Jade Waves Positioning Gap

Jade Waves should not try to out-authority Ashapura on scale claims alone. The better angle is:

- export-focused buyer clarity
- cleaner product qualification flow
- stronger product-page specificity
- Southeast Asia / Gulf / Indian Ocean market relevance
- visible dispatch, packing, and documentation proof

That lets Jade Waves compete on conversion quality and search intent match, not just company size.

## Priority Ranking Strategy

### 1. Own export-buyer intent

Build around phrases such as:

- `quartz sand exporter`
- `feldspar exporter`
- `silica sand supplier for glass`
- `industrial salt exporter`
- `bentonite supplier for foundry / drilling / construction`
- `bulk mineral supplier for Vietnam / UAE / Oman / Philippines`

### 2. Strengthen the 5 priority product pages

The top pages should beat smaller competitors on:

- clearer forms and grades
- chemistry or performance ranges
- packing options
- container/dispatch readiness
- sample and quote CTA
- industries served
- export-market context

### 3. Use market pages carefully

Do not mass-produce thin country pages. Instead:

- keep one strong export-markets hub
- add only a few high-evidence pages where Search Console already shows demand
- make those pages genuinely useful with product fit, packing, shipment flow, and buyer checklist content
