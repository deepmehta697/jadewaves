# Jade Waves Enterprise Website Context

Source website: <https://jadewavesenterprise.com/>

Capture date: 2026-03-21  
Method: live site review via public pages and indexed snippets

## 1. High-level business summary

Jade Waves Enterprise presents itself as a global material solutions company focused on sourcing, processing, and exporting industrial minerals and cementitious materials. The positioning emphasizes reliability, export execution, product quality, and long-term partnerships rather than commodity-only selling.

Core value proposition on the homepage:

- "We are a global material solutions company"
- "We source, process, and sell industrial minerals and cementitious materials at locations worldwide."
- The company frames itself as bridging global industries with "reliable, high-quality raw materials."
- The business claims to serve construction, water treatment, agriculture, metallurgy, manufacturing, ceramics, drilling, glass, cement, and related sectors.

The messaging is strongly export-oriented and trust-oriented. Repeated themes:

- processed at source
- customizable grades
- delivered worldwide
- zero hassle
- sample-first buying
- dependable export partner
- high quality and timely delivery

## 2. Brand and positioning

## 6. Product-specific context found on the site

### Bentonite

The bentonite page describes it as a versatile material primarily composed of montmorillonite, with other minerals such as quartz, feldspar, and gypsum. Claimed properties:

- swelling capacity
- absorbency
- thixotropy
- cation exchange capacity (CEC)

The page appears to market sodium bentonite specifically.

### Silica Sand

The silica sand page emphasizes:

- key properties
- packing options
- monthly capacity
- quality assurance

The search/indexed copy does not expose the full technical specs, but silica sand is clearly a flagship product.

### Fly Ash

The fly ash page contains the most detailed public technical text:

- distinguishes Class F and Class C fly ash
- says they are used in concrete, lightweight aggregate, bricks, roads, and dam construction
- says Jade Waves provides **Class F fly ash**
- claims retention is less than or equal to 15%
- positions the material for sulphate-exposed concrete use cases

### Copper Slag

The copper slag page describes applications in:

- bricks, blocks, and construction materials
- lightweight aggregate in concrete
- replacement for river sand
- abrasive blasting
- partial substitution for silica and hematite in cement raw mixes

It also references NCCBM and suggests up to 5% use in OPC, according to the cited study summary on-page.

### Activated Bleaching Earth

The site describes this product as:

- activated clay used for refining and decolorizing oils, fats, and other industrial products
- derived from bentonite clay
- acid activated for stronger absorptive performance
- intended to improve bleaching efficiency in refinery applications

### Quartz / Quartz Sand

Quartz appears as a major catalog product, though the indexed content available during this review exposed less technical detail than Bentonite/Fly Ash/Copper Slag. It is clearly sold as an industrial mineral in the broader ceramics, glass, and manufacturing context.

### Kaolin / China Clay

Kaolin is listed repeatedly in product navigation and related products, indicating it is an active part of the portfolio, though technical copy was not surfaced clearly in indexed excerpts.

### Salt

Salt is repeatedly included in the product mix and site metadata, but detailed product copy was not clearly surfaced in indexed excerpts during this pass.

### Rubber Granules

Rubber Granules appear in FAQ/product cross-links but are less visible in the main navigation. This suggests either an older or partially surfaced product line.

## 8. Commercial model and buying process

The site gives a fairly clear B2B sales process:

- prospects contact the company with requirements
- the team studies sourcing needs
- the company provides a quote
- free samples are available up to a certain amount, with shipping charged

Commercial details stated in the FAQ:

- Incoterms: FOB, CIF, CNF
- lead time: 7-10 days
- MOQ: accepts LCL/FCL orders
- port/loading context: processing units located near **Mundra Port**
- payment terms:
  - Letter of Credit (L/C) for established clients
  - 30% advance, 70% against scanned BL copy
  - 100% advance for some orders or newer clients

This is strong evidence the site is optimized for inbound export inquiries rather than direct ecommerce.

## 15. Observed website issues and inconsistencies

Several issues are visible on the live/publicly indexed site:

### CMS/account leakage

The homepage exposed account UI elements such as:

- "Sign In"
- "My Account"
- "Signed in as: filler@godaddy.com"

This looks accidental and should not be visible on a public B2B supplier website.

### Repeated / duplicated content blocks

Examples:

- duplicated headings on About page such as repeated "Our Mission" and "Our Commitment"
- repeated product titles in homepage portfolio blocks
- repeated testimonial heading labels

This suggests CMS block duplication or theme rendering issues.

### Product/catalog inconsistency

Different parts of the site list different product sets:

- homepage includes talc, limestone/dolomite, and potash/soda feldspar
- FAQ includes rubber granules
- top navigation on the version indexed at the homepage showed a different set including talc and feldspar
- other pages show a simplified set

This makes it unclear which products are core, current, or secondary.

### Navigation inconsistency

The root page and subpages appear to expose different menu structures in indexed results. Some pages show:

- Why Us?
- Our Founder
- Corporate Brochure
- Track Shipment

while the homepage snapshot surfaced:

- About Us
- Infrastructure
- Company Profile
- Hear from Our CEO

### Thin technical data

Several product pages appear structured for specs but the indexed text does not expose clear data such as:

- particle sizes
- purity ranges
- moisture levels
- packaging weights
- monthly capacity figures
- certifications

The site likely needs stronger spec tables if the goal is high-conversion B2B sourcing.

### Incomplete proof layer

The site includes trust language and testimonials, but limited concrete proof was visible in public copy:

- no surfaced certification details
- no laboratory COAs/spec sheets in public view
- no named client logos
- no visible case studies with metrics

## 16. Best concise summary

Jade Waves Enterprise is positioning itself as an India-based export supplier of industrial minerals and related raw materials, with emphasis on silica sand, bentonite, fly ash, copper slag, quartz, kaolin, salt, and activated bleaching earth. The site is built around trust, export execution, sample-led sales, and global B2B sourcing. Its strongest visible strengths are product breadth, export-oriented commercial details, and founder-led credibility. Its weakest visible areas are CMS cleanliness, duplicated content, inconsistent catalog structure, and limited hard technical/specification proof on some product pages.
